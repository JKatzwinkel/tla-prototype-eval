Collaboration

The „Wörterbuch der ägyptischen Sprache“ (1897–1963) became a great success because Egyptologists from many countries joined forces to build up an extensive archive of paper slips with texts and lemma citations (1.800.000 slips). In a similar way the Thesaurus Linguae Aegyptiae was planned from the start as a collaborative effort. Between its inception in the 1990ies and today many institutions and individual researchers have participated and continue participating in the conception or developement of thesauri of metadata, of text encoding and exchange standards, of standards for hieroglyphic encoding, and most of all in the encoding of countless ancient Egyptian texts in the text database.

In the first phase of the Thesaurus Linguae Aegyptiae primarily institutions and large projects collaborated in the buildup of text corpora (until 2012). From the moment the new text encoding software developed at the Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities became available (from late 2015 onwards), increasingly individual researchers encode their own texts in the database or provide digital data for encoding to the team members in Berlin or Leipzig.

We are very gratefull to every institution and researcher participating in improving and expanding the Thesaurus Linguae Aegyptiae. Every contributor is duly recognised as the author of transcription and translation of his or her text and every text is provided with an individual ID, allowing for precise quotations including all relevant credentials.
