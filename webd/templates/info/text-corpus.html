Text Corpus

The database contains a ever increasing number of texts, organised in smaller and larger corpora that have been defined according to various principles (chronological, topographical, with regard to form, content or social sphere). One ultimate goal of the project is to provide representative corpora of texts from all periods, areas and fields of Egyptian textual production. Originally the database was developed for lexicographical research and priority was given to encoding texts not available in the Wörterbuch der ägyptischen Sprache. Since then, many well known texts have been introduced as well, making the text database a powerful tool for numerous other types of research. Currently the Thesaurus Linguae Aegyptiae contains texts written in hieroglyphs, hieratic and demotic script, in the future texts in coptic script will also be added. The following groups of hieroglyphic and hieratic texts are included:

  • Texts from the Old Kingdom and the First Intermediate Period (tomb inscriptions, specifically those of more recent publications of tombs in Giza, Saqqara, Akhmim, and Deir el-Gebrawi; rock inscriptions; records and notes)
  • Pyramid texts from the Old Kingdom
  • Texts from Old Kingdom archives: The Abu Sir Papyri
  • Biographical texts from the Old Kingdom
  • Letters and Letters to the Dead from the Old Kingdom up to the Third Intermediate Period
  • Literary texts from the Middle Kingdom up to the Late Period (tales; dialogues and lamentations, prophecies; teachings; miscellanies; poetic texts like love songs and hymns; Kemyt)
  • Historical-biographical texts, including royal memorial texts, from the Middle Kingdom and the Second Intermediate Period
  • Royal stelae from the 18th Dynasty
  • Texts from the Amarna Period
  • Medical and magico-medical texts
  • Magical texts from the Middle Kingdom to the New Kingdom
  • The Book of the Dead in several manuscripts from the New Kingdom to the Ptolemaic Period
  • Royal-rhetorical texts from the 19th Dynasty
  • Royal historical-biographical texts from the 25th Dynasty
  • Religious texts from tombs from the 25th and 26th Dynasty
  • Books from the private cult of the dead / late "mortuary liturgies"
  • Books from temple libraries from the ptolemaic and roman times
  • Texts from temples from the ptolemaic and roman times (Dendara, Edfu, Esna, Kom Ombo, small temples in Thebes and Nubia)
  • Stelae from the Egyptian collection in Tübingen

The following groups of demotic texts are included:

  • Demotic literary texts
  • Demotic administrative texts
  • Demotic graffiti and dipinti
  • Demotic legal texts (Codex Hermopolis)
  • Demotic omina and dream texts
  • Demotic inscriptions on objects (including mummy labels)
  • Demotic magical papyri and ostraca
  • Demotic religious texts (Opening the mouth; Book of the Dead; Book of Breathing)
  • Demotic inscriptions on sarcophagi and coffins
  • Demotic school exercises
  • Demotic scientific literature (ethonographical, mathematical, medical, lexicographical)

The database will be continuously augmented in the years to come. Work in Berlin focuses on historical, biographical, funerary and documentary texts from the 1st Millenium BC in hieroglyphic and hieratic script. Work in Leipzig concentrates on scientific literature (medical, magical, divinatory, astronomical, mathematical, geographical, biological, lexicographical).

We thank all external cooperation partners who have been and are willing to provide additional material to the listed corpora and beyond, and we welcome new partners who would like to contribute their material.
